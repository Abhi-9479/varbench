Gene: PCSK9 (proprotein convertase subtilisin/kexin type 9)

Function summary:
- PCSK9 protein binds the LDL receptor (LDLR) on hepatocytes and targets it
  for lysosomal degradation.
- Higher PCSK9 activity therefore reduces hepatic LDLR availability and raises
  circulating LDL cholesterol (LDL-C).

Population-genetic findings:
- Carriers of loss-of-function (LOF) variants in PCSK9 (e.g. p.Tyr142X, p.Cys679X,
  p.Arg46Leu) have approximately 28-50% lower plasma LDL-C than non-carriers.
- A landmark cohort study (Cohen et al., NEJM 2006) reported that African-American
  carriers of PCSK9 LOF variants had an approximately 88% reduction in risk of
  coronary heart disease over 15 years of follow-up; a smaller but significant
  reduction was observed in European-ancestry carriers of p.R46L.
- No homozygous LOF carrier has been reported with any apparent adverse phenotype
  beyond very low LDL-C.

Question for the analyst:
Given these human-genetics findings, a pharmaceutical company developing a drug
targeting PCSK9 must choose between three mechanism-of-action (MoA) strategies:
  - 'agonist'   : a drug that increases PCSK9 activity
  - 'inhibitor' : a drug that decreases PCSK9 activity
  - 'unclear'   : the human-genetics evidence does not favor either direction
Which MoA does the population-genetic evidence above support?
