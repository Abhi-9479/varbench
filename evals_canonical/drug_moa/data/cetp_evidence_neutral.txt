Gene: CETP (cholesteryl ester transfer protein)

Function:
- CETP mediates exchange of cholesteryl esters and triglycerides between
  HDL and apoB-containing lipoproteins (LDL, VLDL).
- Genetic loss of CETP activity is associated with elevated HDL-C and
  modestly reduced LDL-C.

Carrier-versus-noncarrier observations from published cohorts:
- Common East Asian variant p.Asp442Gly: +13% HDL-C, -4% LDL-C.
  Reported CHD odds ratios across published meta-analyses: 0.93, 0.97,
  1.04, 0.89, 1.02 (six independent studies, no formal pooled estimate
  available in this brief).
- European TaqIB B2 allele (intronic, partial-LOF tag): +8% HDL-C.
  Reported CHD effect: meta-analytic OR 0.95 (95% CI 0.90-1.00).
- Rare homozygous CETP-deficient probands (Japan, n~30 published cases):
  HDL-C 2-6x population mean. CHD outcomes are heterogeneous; some
  kindreds report increased atherosclerotic burden despite high HDL-C,
  others report apparent longevity.
- Mendelian randomization analyses using CETP variants as instruments
  for HDL-C yield estimates that are not consistently distinguishable
  from zero effect on CHD across different variant sets and outcome
  definitions.

Clinical-trial observations (drug-class evidence, included as additional
data for the analyst to weigh):
- torcetrapib (CETP inhibitor + raised HDL-C +72%): increased all-cause
  mortality in ILLUMINATE (HR 1.58), terminated.
- dalcetrapib (CETP inhibitor, weaker on LDL): dal-OUTCOMES neutral
  on CHD, terminated.
- evacetrapib (CETP inhibitor, larger lipid effect): ACCELERATE neutral
  on CHD, terminated.
- anacetrapib (CETP inhibitor, longest exposure): REVEAL HR 0.91 for
  major coronary events; not pursued commercially despite the signal.
